Hi.

I'm Ganesh.

I study Electronics and Communication Engineering.

Most days you'll find me reading papers about oscillators, trying to understand why circuits misbehave, building FPGA projects that become much larger than they were supposed to be, or writing notes that eventually turn into entire books.

Outside engineering I enjoy old-film photography, long walks, devotional music, and occasionally writing about things completely unrelated to semiconductors.

This website is simply my notebook on the internet.

"This is where I document things I'm learning, things I've built, and things I'm still trying to understand."

About

I grew up fascinated by physics and electronics.

Over time that curiosity drifted toward analog circuits, digital hardware, verification, and the software that connects them.

I like projects that sit at the intersection of mathematics, hardware and programming.

When I'm not debugging RTL or reading papers, I enjoy old-film photography, devotional music, and collecting interesting books.

This website exists because I wanted one place where I could publish everything I'm learning.
